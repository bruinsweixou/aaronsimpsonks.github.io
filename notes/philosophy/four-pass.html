The following method of studying philosophy was originally outlined in Susan Rigetti's So You Want to Study Philosophy... guide. I'm copying it here for reasons of personal reference and signal-boosting, as I think this method is incredibly valuable for studying and understanding any new subject.

The way it works is very simple - read everything four times:

  • Once casual pass for general observation. The goal here is just to gain a simple map of the terrain.
  • Next pass is more focused. Take notes. Read to understand. Be able to determine what the author actually means by what they're saying.
  • Third pass is for engagement and critique. Can you think of any arguments and examples, as well as counter-arguments and counter-examples?
  • Fourth and last read is a cooldown read. Take notice of how you feel about and understand the text after deconstruction and engagement.
