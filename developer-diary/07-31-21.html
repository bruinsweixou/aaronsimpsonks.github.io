Welcome to the first developer diary! I don’t know if I’ll have the mental fortitude to do these daily, but I want them to be somewhat regular and frequent. The goal here is to be casual, free-flowing, and conversational. Sometimes we’ll deal with heavy topics in the news, while others might just be what I ate for breakfast that day. Unlike other articles, these will probably never be edited after they are published as I think that kind of defeats the point of a diary entry.

Today in America

According to some notes released by the Justice Department, Donald Trump asked DOJ officials to call the 2020 election “corrupt” in an attempt to delegitimize the results. The notes were part of a House Oversight Committee investigation.

The above link comes from a Fox News article, so you can’t really claim “liberal bias” on this one - although if you still believe Trump won the 2020 election, you’re so far down the rabbit hole I don’t even know why you’re here. Still, I’d like to read the full report and see what is sensationalized versus what isn’t.

A particular line in the article that stands out to me is the one where Trump criticizes DOJ officials for “not following the Internet the way I do”. When we talk about the actions of the Trump administration, as well as debate the authoritarian nature of Trump himself, there’s a tendency to paint it as an evil mastermind leading the misinformed masses to the gallows. However, this quote confirms a frightening realization I had later on in Trump’s presidency - the man is just that stupid. In reality, Trump consumed the same conspiratorial blogspam his followers did. Tucker Carlson and Breitbart News may have had more influence over the President of the United States than the President had over them. Just sit on that one for a bit.

This Week in IPOs

The investing app Robinhood had a particularly sour IPO this week with causes pointing towards investor confusion and overall suspicion after the whole GME controversy. It leads one to question why the company would choose to IPO now as compared to later on down the line when the issue is less fresh in people’s heads, or after an exonerating SEC investigation. Still, I’m not the CEO of a billion-dollar company so I’m likely missing something.
