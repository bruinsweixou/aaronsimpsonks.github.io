Climate Anxiety, Simple Living, and Happiness

These were going to be three separate sections, but they’re all interconnected enough to warrant one big section that links them all.

I try not to follow climate science too much, as it tends to dramatically increase my anxiety and fear for the future. I’m content acknowledging the reality, trying to live a more sustainable life, and advocating for politicians that will push beneficial policies like carbon taxes and renewable energy. However, sometimes it’s just too hard to ignore and some of it popped up in my Twitter feed last night. Noah Smith retweeted a thread discussing the effects of 3 degrees Celsius of warming and why it’s essential to prevent it. The typical waves of anxiety came along, but also questions about how to manage it and what my personal options are. In a world where climate change is likely going to be a reality, how can I survive?

The first thought that popped into my head is the idea of “environment-independent happiness”. For most people, our environment plays a significant role in our moods like happiness. Some of this is attached to basic things like food, water, and shelter, but a lot of it also comes from things like people’s validation of us.

While some of this is unavoidable, it’s possible to have too much of your happiness invested in these external sources. Since you’re not omnipotent, you are trusting your happiness to be bent and shaped by forces that you can’t control, which I personally don’t like. If your mind is a book, high investment of this kind is giving others a pen and letting them write the pages. No bueno.

It’s important to have a certain fragment of your happiness independent and self-generated from within. This is hard to do and you have to sort of fake it at first, but it’s absolutely possible. In this way, you can insulate yourself from the turbulence and change of the outside world. Even if I was to lose my job tomorrow, or get evicted, or lose a family member, I’d still have a baseline level of contentment and energy to keep me going. Mindset absolutely plays a role, and I think whether or not I’m happy in a climate-changed world 20 or 30 years from now is a choice I can make.

Alongside a shift in mindset can also come physical preparations. I’m not gonna go full shotgun shells and frozen food, but I think a degree of simple living is worth exploring. I’m already an incredibly minimalist person, so it’s in my nature to a degree. Things here would align closely with the traditional “sustainable” lifestyle - smaller apartment, walk or bike as much as possible, shift diet, learn to grow food and cook, unplugged days, etc.

Like all living creatures, human beings can adapt. In fact, humans are uniquely capable of adaptation due to our intelligence and self-awareness. Climate anxiety doesn’t have to mean eternal depression. Take joy in everything that you can and push for those making real change, but also prepare.

A World Without Privacy

This is going to be written into a separate article eventually, but over the course of debating with my friends last night, one of them voiced the opinion that they didn’t think privacy was a fundamental right. Needless to say, this caused quite the stir. The notion of a world without privacy sounds completely unsustainable, but I can’t quite yet articulate why. All I know is, I can write about it for a long time.

A particular argument brought up was that we don’t live in a private world right now with corporations like Facebook and Google. While that is true to some extent, until the government starts mandating that we poop with the door open, I think there’s still a fight for privacy to be had.

Another one was the common refrain of “if you have nothing to hide, you don’t need privacy.” I’d hope by now everyone recognizes why this is absurd - again, pooping with the door open. It also eventually verged into the topic of data leaks and encryption backdoors for law enforcement.

Many words to be written. Many thoughts to be had.
